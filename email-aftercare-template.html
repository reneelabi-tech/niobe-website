Facial Aftercare

To get the most from your treatment and protect your skin over the next 24–48 hours:

  • Avoid touching or washing your face for at least 4 hours after your treatment
  • Keep skin hydrated — apply your regular moisturiser or the one recommended by your therapist
  • Avoid direct sun exposure for 48 hours and apply SPF before going outdoors
  • If you had an active treatment or peel, avoid retinol, AHAs and BHAs for 5–7 days
  • Avoid heavy makeup for 24 hours to allow skin to breathe and settle
  • Drink plenty of water throughout the day
  • Some temporary redness or sensitivity is completely normal and will ease within a few hours
  • If you had extractions, keep the area clean and apply a light soothing serum — avoid touching it

Massage Aftercare

To support your body as it continues to respond to the treatment:

  • Drink plenty of water to help flush toxins released during the massage
  • Rest where possible — the body continues to process the treatment after you leave
  • Avoid strenuous exercise for 24 hours
  • If oils were used, wait a few hours before showering to allow them to absorb fully
  • Mild muscle soreness is normal, especially after deep tissue work — it will ease within 48 hours
  • Avoid alcohol for the rest of the day
  • Apply a warm compress to any areas of tension if needed

Body Treatment Aftercare

Your skin has just been deeply nourished. To extend the results:

  • Avoid showering for at least 4–6 hours after a wrap to allow the ingredients to continue absorbing
  • Apply a rich body moisturiser within 24 hours to lock in hydration
  • Drink plenty of water throughout the day
  • Avoid hot baths, saunas or steam rooms for 24 hours
  • Wear loose, comfortable clothing after your treatment to avoid irritating freshly treated skin
  • Some temporary flushing or warmth in the skin is completely normal

Waxing Aftercare

To keep skin calm and prevent irritation or ingrown hairs:

  • Avoid touching the waxed area for at least 24 hours
  • No hot baths, saunas, steam rooms or swimming pools for 24 hours
  • Avoid direct sun exposure on waxed areas for 48 hours — apply SPF if needed
  • Wear loose clothing to prevent friction and irritation
  • Begin gentle exfoliation 48–72 hours after treatment to help prevent ingrown hairs
  • Avoid perfumed products, deodorants or tanning products on waxed areas for 24 hours
  • Some redness and sensitivity is normal and will settle quickly

Brows & Lashes Aftercare

To protect your results and keep everything looking its best:

  • Avoid water, steam or oil-based products near the eye area for 24–48 hours
  • Do not rub, pull or pick at lashes or brows
  • If you had a tint, avoid direct sun exposure for 24 hours
  • For lash extensions: brush gently daily with a clean spoolie, sleep on your back where possible, and avoid oil-based makeup removers around the eyes
  • For brow lamination: brush brows upward each morning and apply a nourishing brow serum regularly
  • Avoid waterproof mascara on extensions as it weakens the adhesive bond

Hammam Aftercare

Your skin has been deeply cleansed and your body thoroughly relaxed. To carry that feeling forward:

  • Apply a rich moisturiser to the body — skin will be particularly receptive to hydration after the ceremony
  • Drink plenty of water to rehydrate fully
  • Avoid sun exposure for 24 hours as freshly exfoliated skin is more sensitive
  • Rest for the remainder of the day where possible — the relaxation effect continues for several hours
  • Avoid strenuous exercise and alcohol for the rest of the day
  • A gentle moisturising routine over the following days will help maintain the softness and clarity of the skin

Thermal Aftercare

Your body has been through a gentle but effective thermal experience. Give it time to settle:

  • Drink plenty of water to rehydrate — this is the most important step
  • Rest where possible for the remainder of the day
  • Avoid strenuous exercise and alcohol for at least a few hours
  • A light, nourishing meal is ideal — avoid anything heavy straight after
  • Apply moisturiser to the body as the skin will be more open and receptive after heat exposure

After Your Visit

A few things to help you get the most from your treatment:

  • Allow any nail polish or gel to set fully before handling objects or getting hands wet
  • Apply cuticle oil regularly over the coming days to keep nails and skin nourished
  • Avoid prolonged water exposure for the first few hours
  • Drink plenty of water throughout the day
  • If you have any concerns about your treatment or skin response, please do not hesitate to contact us
